# Training

    ## Lesson debrief (3 wins / 3 fixes / next objective)
    **Use when:** After a training flight, you want a structured debrief.  
    **Tags:** debrief, maneuvers, plan

    ```text
    You are my instructor debriefing a GA training flight.

Student level: [PPL / IR / CPL]
Maneuvers covered: [LIST]
What went well: [NOTES]
What was weak: [NOTES]

Create a structured debrief with:
- 3 wins
- 3 fixes (with a drill plan)
- 1 safety item
- Next lesson objective and homework
Keep it practical and specific.
    ```

    ## ACS standards mapping
    **Use when:** You want to map a lesson to ACS and identify gaps.  
    **Tags:** acs, standards, training

    ```text
    Act as a CFI.

Given this lesson summary:
[PASTE]

Output:
- Which ACS areas it most closely maps to (by topic, not exact numbers)
- What typical common errors apply
- 5 targeted drills to close gaps
Do not pretend to quote the ACS verbatim; summarize conservatively.
    ```

    ## Chair-flying script generator
    **Use when:** You want a repeatable chair-flying routine.  
    **Tags:** chair-fly, flows, checklists

    ```text
    You are my CFI. Create a chair-flying script for:
- Aircraft: [TYPE]
- Scenario: [normal takeoff, short field, go-around, emergency, etc.]
- Airport environment: [towered/non-towered]
- Emphasis: [flows, callouts, checklist discipline]

Include:
- Setup (what to visualize)
- Callouts sequence
- Decision points
- Common errors to avoid
Keep it 10 minutes or less.
    ```

    ## Confidence builder for a nervous student
    **Use when:** You’re coaching a student who gets anxious (turbulence, landings, etc.).  
    **Tags:** student, coaching, confidence

    ```text
    Act as an empathetic but direct CFI.

Student anxiety trigger: [turbulence/landings/ATC/etc.]
Observed behavior: [NOTES]
Goal: [specific]

Create:
- A short explanation that normalizes the feeling without minimizing it
- 3 practical tools for the next flight
- A graded exposure plan for the next 2–3 lessons
Keep it concrete and aviation-specific.
    ```

    ## Oral prep: rapid-fire questions (PPL/IR/CPL)
    **Use when:** You want to practice oral exam style Q&A.  
    **Tags:** oral, prep, checkride

    ```text
    Act as a DPE.

Rating: [PPL / IR / CPL]
Emphasis areas: [weather / airspace / regs / systems / performance / IFR]
Ask me 15 questions, one at a time.
After each answer, grade it and correct me if needed.
Keep it realistic and fair.
    ```
